## 2️⃣ Look Inside the Book Itself

> **Front‑matter** (the first few pages) is the most reliable place to find an agent’s name.

| What to Scan | Where |
|--------------|-------|
| **Copyright page** – often says “Literary Agent: [Name] – [Agency] – [Phone/Email]”. | Usually on the verso (back) of the title page. |
| **Acknowledgements / Thanks** – authors sometimes thank their agent. | Near the back of the book. |
| **Author’s bio** (often on the back cover or dust‑jacket) – may list “represented by”. | Back cover, inside flap, or on a separate author’s page. |
| **ISBN & publisher data** – can be used for a quick lookup on rights‑databases. | Inside front matter or on the copyright page. |

**How to access if you don’t own a copy:**  

- **Google Books** (books.google.com) – many titles have a “Preview” that includes the copyright page.  
- **Amazon “Look Inside”** – often shows the first 10–15 pages.  
- **Internet Archive** (archive.org) – some books are scanned in full.  
- **Local library** – request a physical copy or a scanned page via interlibrary loan.

---

## 3️⃣ Check the Publisher’s Rights or Publicity Page

Publishers almost always list a **rights/contact email** for inquiries about foreign, film, or audio rights. While they may not give you the agent’s name directly, they’ll forward a request or at least confirm who handles the author’s rights.

| Publisher | Where to Look |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Rowman & Littlefield** | www.rowman.com → *Authors & Rights* (usually a “Rights@rowman.com”). |
| **Springer** | www.springernature.com → *Authors & Editors* → *Contact Rights*. |
| **HarperCollins** | www.harpercollins.com → *Rights Department* (often a generic email). |

**Sample email template** (you can copy‑paste and customize):

```
Subject: Inquiry about representation for Charles A. Cerami

Dear Rights Team,

I’m writing to inquire about the literary representation for Charles A. Cerami, author of “[Title]” (ISBN: [xxx‑xxxx‑xxxx]). I’m interested in discussing [your purpose – e.g., translation rights, adaptation, speaking engagement] and would appreciate being directed to the appropriate agent or rights contact.

Thank you for your assistance.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Organization]
[Phone] | [Email]
```

Publishers typically respond within a few days, especially if you’re a rights professional.

## 4️⃣ Search Professional Literary‑Agent Directories

| Directory | What it Offers | Access |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| **Publishers Marketplace** (PM) | Deals database, recent contract announcements (includes agent names). | Subscription (≈$30/mo) – free trial available. |
| **Literary Marketplace** (LMP) | Printed/online directory of agents, editors, publishers. | Library copy or purchase (≈$30). |
| **AgentQuery.com** | Free searchable database of agents (by genre, location). | Free. |
| **QueryTracker.com** | Community‑driven listings, author–agent matches. | Free/paid tiers. |
| **Association of Authors’ Representatives (AAR)** | Only vetted agents; searchable by genre. | Free (list is public). |

**How to use them for Cerami:**

1. **Enter the author’s name** → see if any agent is listed as “represented”.  
2. **Enter the book title** → some deals are logged with the agent’s name.  
3. **Filter by genre** (e.g., *Non‑fiction / Health & Wellness*, *Environmental Science*) – helps narrow down agents who specialize in his niche.

*Tip:* If you spot an agent who recently closed a deal for a *Springer* non‑fiction title, that agent is a strong candidate.

## 7️⃣ Contact the Author Directly (If All Else Fails)

If the above steps come up empty, a polite outreach to the author himself/herself is acceptable—especially if you’re a rights professional, a fellow writer, or a researcher.

**Sample outreach email:**

```
Subject: Inquiry Regarding Representation for Charles A. Cerami

Dear Mr./Ms. Cerami,

My name is [Your Name] and I’m a [your role – e.g., literary‑rights manager at XYZ Publishing, literary scout, researcher]. I’m interested in discussing potential [translation/film/audio/foreign] rights for your book “[Title]” (ISBN: [xxxx]).

Would you be willing to share the name and contact details of your literary agent, or direct me to the appropriate person to speak with?

Thank you for your time, and congratulations on the success of your recent work.

Warm regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Organization]
[Phone] | [Email]
```

**Best practice:**  
- Keep the email short (3–4 sentences).  
- Mention why you’re contacting (e.g., “I represent a European publisher interested in a French translation”).  
- Use a professional signature.
