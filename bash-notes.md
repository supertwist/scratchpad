**Bash (short for “Bourne‑Again Shell”) is a simple, text‑based way to talk to your computer.**  
- You type short commands (like “open this file” or “move these pictures”) and Bash tells the operating system what to do.  
- It’s the command‑line interface that comes built‑in to most Linux and macOS computers, acting like a translator between your words and the computer’s actions.  
- Besides running one‑off commands, Bash can chain many steps together in a script, letting you automate repetitive tasks—much like a macro you’d record in a spreadsheet, but for the whole system.  

In short, Bash lets you control the computer by typing instructions instead of clicking icons.
