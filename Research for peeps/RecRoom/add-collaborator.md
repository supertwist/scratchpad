## Yes – You can add collaborators to a YouTube channel  
You just have to use a **Brand Account** (or a Brand‑Account‑linked channel) and then assign the appropriate **permissions** to the people you want to work with.

Below is a complete, step‑by‑step guide covering:

| What you need to know | How to do it | When to use each role |
|-----------------------|--------------|-----------------------|
| **Brand Account** – the container that lets multiple Google users manage a single channel without sharing passwords. | Create a Brand Account or convert an existing channel to one. | Use for any channel that will have more than one manager (marketing team, video editors, agencies, etc.). |
| **Permissions/Roles** – Owner, Manager, Editor, Communications Manager, Viewer. | Add users in YouTube Studio → Settings → Permissions. | Choose the role that matches the tasks each person will perform. |
| **Limits** – Only the **Owner** can delete the channel or transfer ownership. Managers can upload, edit, view analytics, but can’t delete the channel. | Keep at least one Owner (usually you). | For agencies or freelancers, give **Manager** or **Editor** access, not Owner. |
| **Two‑step verification (2FA)** – Required for anyone with a Manager or higher role. | Ask collaborators to enable 2FA on their Google accounts. | Improves security for business assets. |

## 1️⃣ Set Up (or Convert) Your Channel to a Brand Account

### A. If you’re starting a brand‑new channel

1. **Log into YouTube** with the Google account that will own the channel.  
2. Click your **profile picture** ► **Create a channel**.  
3. Choose **“Use a custom name”** → type your business name.  
4. Click **Create** – YouTube automatically creates a **Brand Account** behind the scenes.

### B. If you already have a personal channel you want to share

1. Go to **YouTube → Settings** (gear icon in the left‑hand menu).  
2. Under **“Account”**, click **“Add or manage your channel(s)”**.  
3. Click **“Create a new channel”** → select **Brand Account**.  
4. Follow the prompts to give the new Brand Account a name (your business name).  
5. Once created, you can **move existing videos** from your personal channel to the brand channel (YouTube will ask you to confirm).  

> **Tip:** Keep the original personal channel as a “test” or “personal” space; never upload business content there.

## 2️⃣ Invite Collaborators & Assign Roles

### Step‑by‑step

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Open Permissions** | In YouTube, click **Your profile picture → YouTube Studio**.<br>In the left menu, go to **Settings** (bottom left) → **Permissions**. |
| **2. Manage Permissions** | Click **“Invite”** (or the **“+”** button). |
| **3. Enter Email** | Type the Google‑account email of the person you want to add (must be a Google account – e.g., `john.doe@gmail.com` or `john@yourcompany.com`). |
| **4. Choose Role** | Select from the dropdown: **Owner**, **Manager**, **Editor**, **Communications Manager**, **Viewer**. |
| **5. Send Invitation** | Click **Invite**. The person receives an email to accept the invitation. |
| **6. Acceptance** | The collaborator clicks the link, signs into their Google account, and the invitation is added to their account. |
| **7. Verify** | Back in **Permissions**, you’ll now see the new user listed with the role you assigned. |

### Roles – What Each Can Do

| Role | Permissions | Typical Use |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| **Owner** | Full control – add/remove owners, delete channel, transfer ownership. | Founder/CEO, or a trusted senior manager. |
| **Manager** | Upload videos, edit channel info, view and export analytics, manage playlists, moderate comments. **Cannot** delete the channel or transfer ownership. | Marketing manager, content strategist, agency partner. |
| **Editor** | Edit video details (title, description, tags), add cards/end screens, moderate comments. **Cannot** upload new videos or view analytics. | Copy editor, SEO specialist. |
| **Communications Manager** | Manage comments, respond to community posts, moderate live chat. | Community manager, social media rep. |
| **Viewer** | View analytics and channel performance data only. | Executives, investors, or anyone who needs reporting but not editing rights. |

> **Best practice:** Keep **Owner** count to **1–2** people. Give everyone else **Manager** or lower. This reduces the risk of accidental channel deletion.

## 3️⃣ Security Checklist for Collaborators

| Action | Why it matters | How to enforce |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Two‑Factor Authentication (2FA)** | Prevents unauthorized access if credentials are compromised. | In Google Account → Security → 2‑Step Verification. Require every Manager/Owner to enable it. |
| **Strong, unique passwords** | Reduces risk of password‑reuse attacks. | Encourage use of a password manager (e.g., LastPass, 1Password). |
| **Regular permission audit** | Removes stale accounts (former employees, contractors). | Quarterly: YouTube Studio → Settings → Permissions → Review list, remove inactive users. |
| **Signed NDA / Contract** | Protects proprietary content and brand assets. | Have collaborators sign an agreement before granting access. |
| **IP whitelisting (optional)** | Limits login attempts to known locations. | Set up via Google Workspace if your organization uses it. |

## 4️⃣ Advanced Collaboration Options

| Feature | When it’s useful | How to set it up |
|---------|------------------|------------------|
| **Channel Transfer** | You’re selling the business or handing the channel to a new owner. | Owner → Settings → Permissions → Click the **“Transfer ownership”** icon next to a Manager’s name. The new owner must accept. |
| **Multiple Managers with Different Focus Areas** | You have a large team (e.g., separate video production and community teams). | Create separate Manager accounts: **Video‑Production‑Manager** (uploads/edits) and **Community‑Manager** (comments, live chat). |
| **Agency Access via Google Workspace** | You work with a marketing agency that uses a corporate Google domain. | Add the agency’s domain as a **“trusted domain”** in Google Admin, then invite agency emails as Managers. |
| **Third‑Party Scheduling Tools (e.g., Buffer, Later)** | You want to schedule videos ahead of time without giving full channel access. | Some tools support YouTube publishing via OAuth – you grant the tool **Manager** rights, not the agency. |
| **Brand‑Account‑Linked Google Business Profile** | You want your channel’s name, logo, and contact info to appear automatically on Google Search/Maps. | In Google Business Profile, claim the brand account as “Owner”. This syncs contact details. |

## 5️⃣ Common Issues & How to Fix Them

| Issue | Likely Cause | Fix |
|-------|--------------|-----|
| **Invited collaborator never receives the email** | Sent to a non‑Google address, or spam filter blocks it. | Verify the email is a Google account; ask them to check spam. Resend the invitation. |
| **Collaborator can’t upload videos** | Assigned **Editor** instead of **Manager**. | Change role to **Manager** (or higher). |
| **“Access denied” when trying to edit channel settings** | The user is still a **Viewer** or **Communications Manager**. | Upgrade role to **Manager** or **Owner**. |
| **Channel disappears from YouTube Studio after adding a new Owner** | The new Owner removed the original Owner accidentally. | Only Owners can remove other Owners; make sure you retain at least one Owner you control. |
| **Analytics data shows “Direct” traffic spikes after adding a Manager** | The new Manager may be manually sharing video links (e.g., via email) which registers as Direct. | Not a problem—just be aware of source changes. |

## 6️⃣ Quick “Add a Collaborator” Cheat Sheet

1. **Open YouTube Studio → Settings → Permissions**  
2. Click **Invite** → Enter collaborator’s Google email.  
3. Choose **Manager** (or other role).  
4. Click **Invite** → collaborator accepts email.  
5. **Verify** they appear in the list.  

**Remember:**  
- **2FA** on every Manager/Owner account.  
- **Audit** permissions every 30‑90 days.  
- Keep **Owner** count low.

## 7️⃣ TL;DR Summary

- **Yes**, you can add collaborators.  
- Use a **Brand Account** (or convert your channel to one).  
- Assign **Permissions** (Owner, Manager, Editor, etc.) via YouTube Studio → Settings → Permissions.  
- Enforce **2FA** and strong passwords for security.  
- Review and prune access regularly.
