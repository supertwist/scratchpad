## Tool Comparison Table

| Tool | Link | Difficulty & Analysis |
|------|------|----------------------|
| **3D Mold Maker** | [moldmaker.vercel.app](https://moldmaker.vercel.app/) | **Beginner** — The simplest option. Upload an STL in your browser, configure wall thickness and split axis, and download a two-part mold STL. Zero CAD knowledge required. Free tier available; Pro is $12/month. Best for: anyone who wants a mold file in minutes with no learning curve. |
| **MoldMaker AI** | [moldmakerai.com](https://moldmakerai.com/) | **Beginner** — AI-powered web tool that automatically determines optimal split planes, alignment keys, and draft angles. One-click workflow with no manual decisions. Slightly more automated than 3D Mold Maker but less manual control. Best for: users who want the tool to make all engineering decisions for them. |
| **Action BOX Mold Tool** | [mold.actionbox.ca](https://mold.actionbox.ca/) | **Beginner** — Browser-based, no signup required. Auto-generates parting planes with color-coded demoldability visualization, plus sprue and vent channels. Exports to STL, OBJ, 3MF, or STEP. More feature-rich than the other beginner tools while remaining approachable. Designed for desktop injection machines like the INJEKTO 3. Best for: makers and educators who want guidance on draft angles without leaving the browser. |
| **DevStl Tools** | [devcad.com/eng/devstltools.asp](https://www.devcad.com/eng/devstltools.asp) | **Beginner–Intermediate** — Free Windows desktop app with a step-by-step mold wizard. Provides boolean operations (union, subtract, intersect) and auto-generates alignment holes. Also includes STL repair and manipulation tools. More hands-on than web tools but guided by a wizard. Best for: hobbyist CNC/3D-printer users who want more control without full CAD complexity. |
| **Meshmixer** | [Autodesk App Store](https://apps.autodesk.com/FUSION/en/Detail/Index?id=4108920185261935100&appLang=en&os=Win64) | **Beginner–Intermediate** — Free Autodesk mesh editor. Create molds via Boolean Difference (subtract part from bounding box). Also handles mesh repair, hollowing, and hole-filling. No longer actively developed (v3.5), but extensive tutorial ecosystem. Requires understanding of boolean operations but the interface is straightforward. Best for: users comfortable with basic 3D manipulation who need mesh-level control. |
| **Blender** | [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blender_(software)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blender_(software)) | **Intermediate** — Free, open-source 3D suite. Mold creation uses the Boolean Difference modifier on imported STL geometry. More powerful and flexible than Meshmixer (supports exact solver, modifiers, scripting) but has a notoriously steep initial learning curve. Many mold-making tutorials available. Best for: users already familiar with Blender or willing to invest time learning a general-purpose 3D tool. |
| **FreeCAD** | [freecad.org](https://www.freecad.org/) | **Intermediate** — Free, open-source parametric CAD. Import STL, convert to solid, use Part Design workbench for booleans, draft angles, and parting lines. Parametric history means you can go back and adjust dimensions. Steeper than mesh-based tools but teaches real CAD fundamentals. Best for: users who want to learn parametric modeling and need STEP/IGES export for manufacturing. |
| **Autodesk Fusion 360** | [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autodesk_Fusion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autodesk_Fusion) | **Intermediate–Professional** — Cloud/desktop CAD with dedicated mold design tools: parting line creation, core/cavity extraction, and Moldflow simulation integration. Converts STL meshes to solid bodies. Free for personal use; ~$545/year commercial. The best balance of accessibility and professional capability. Best for: serious hobbyists and professionals who want an all-in-one design-to-simulation workflow. |
| **Cadmould Flex** | [simcon.ai — Cadmould](https://www.simcon.ai/en-us/solutions/cadmould-plastic-injection-molding-simulation-software/) | **Professional** — Injection molding simulation platform with filling, packing, warpage, and thermal analysis. Includes Varimos AI for automated cause-and-effect analysis. Designed to be approachable for non-specialists despite professional depth. Five subscription tiers with a free 14-day trial. Best for: engineers validating mold designs before committing to tooling. |
| **Moldex3D** | [moldex3d.com](https://www.moldex3d.com/) | **Professional** — World-leading injection molding CAE. Supports standard, gas-assisted, co-injection, and compression molding simulation. Imports from CATIA, Creo, NX, SOLIDWORKS, and STL. Industry solutions for automotive, medical, optics. Requires training to use effectively. Best for: manufacturing teams running production mold validation. |
| **Autodesk Moldflow** | [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moldflow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moldflow) | **Professional** — Industry-standard simulation suite (Adviser for guidance, Insight for deep analysis). 11,000+ material grades in database. Covers gas-assisted, microcellular, co-injection, and compression molding. The most comprehensive but also the most complex and expensive tool on this list. Best for: large-scale manufacturing operations requiring rigorous mold validation and optimization. |

## Summary

The **most user-friendly option** is **3D Mold Maker** (or **MoldMaker AI** if you prefer a fully automated approach) — both are browser-based, require no CAD skills, and produce a usable mold file in minutes. For users who want more control without professional software, **Action BOX** and **Meshmixer** offer a good middle ground. For production-quality mold engineering, **Fusion 360** provides the best on-ramp to professional tools.
